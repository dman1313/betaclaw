# International Move Research and Quote

Use this skill to produce immediately usable moving research for cross-border household relocations.

## Core workflow

1. Clarify the move profile if missing:
   - origin city/country
   - destination city/country
   - target move window
   - shipment size estimate
   - special items (bicycle, table, art, electronics, vehicle, pets)
   - whether storage is needed
   - whether residence/visa status is already known

2. Do at least two research passes:
   - Pass 1: broad market scan for movers serving the route
   - Pass 2: verify shortlisted movers via official contact pages, route pages, branch pages, or customs pages

3. Prefer movers with one or more of these signs of fit:
   - official quote form or clear contact path
   - international household-goods specialization
   - stated customs-clearance support
   - storage and insurance options
   - groupage, consolidated move, shared container, MoveCube, LCL, or small-move language
   - office presence or agent network near origin or destination

4. Separate facts from assumptions:
   - Facts: what the company explicitly states, plus verified office/contact details
   - Assumptions: inferred suitability, likely service fit, likely pricing posture, likely operational model

5. Do not invent prices, transit times, customs exemptions, or service availability.

## Research standards

- Prioritize official company websites over directories.
- Use directories only as secondary support when official contact details are incomplete.
- If a site blocks fetches, keep the company only if another official page or a high-confidence listing supports the contact path.
- Capture source URLs for every mover.
- Note when a contact route is form-only instead of direct email.

## What to capture per mover

- company name
- website
- source URL(s)
- origin-side contact or nearest office
- destination-side contact or nearest office
- phone
- email if publicly listed
- contact form availability
- services explicitly stated
- evidence of small-load suitability (groupage, consolidated, shared container, small moves, crate system)
- customs support
- storage support
- insurance support
- professionalism signals (years in business, accreditations, network size, testimonials only if clearly on official site)
- concise fit assessment for this exact move

## Output structure

Return these sections when requested:

1. Executive Summary
2. Recommended Shipping Approach
3. Shortlist of Movers
4. Outreach Email Templates
5. Company-Specific Email Variants
6. Quote Comparison Table
7. Client Quote Request Pack
8. Document and Customs Watchouts
9. Action Plan
10. Skill Conversion (if asked)

## Recommended comparison criteria

Use simple, decision-friendly fields:

- route fit
- smaller-load suitability
- household-goods experience
- customs support
- storage
- insurance
- contact quality
- confidence level
- notes / follow-up questions

## Email drafting guidance

Draft one general outreach email and then short variants tailored to each mover.

Include:
- route
- move window
- load size estimate
- notable items
- request for survey/video survey
- request for shared-container/LCL/groupage options where relevant
- request for customs guidance and required documents
- request for insurance options and storage pricing if needed

Keep tone professional and concise.

## Customs and document watchouts

Summarize only what was supported in research.

Typical buckets to verify:
- passport / visa / residence status
- presence in destination country at clearance
- proof goods are used household effects
- inventory requirements
- treatment of new items
- restricted/prohibited goods
- treatment of bicycles, wood items, electronics, alcohol, food, medicines
- duties/taxes when exemptions do not apply

Mark anything not fully verified as “verify with mover and Malaysian customs/broker”.

## Final QA

Before finishing, verify:
- at least 4 credible movers unless the route is unusually thin
- all requested sections are present
- no fabricated price claims
- assumptions are labeled
- contact paths are actionable now
